2. Qualitative Summary

**Key Strategic Initiatives and Guidances (2025):**

  • Capital Returns: Citi maintained a 74% payout ratio in 1Q2025 ($2.8B returned to shareholders via dividends and buybacks), reflecting confidence in long-term U.S. economic stability and dollar reserve status. Guidance suggests continued prioritization of capital recycling under the $20B plan.
  • Margin Optimization: Operating expenses declined 5% YoY in 1Q2025, driven by cost-cutting in legal, severance, and technology. CEO emphasized "positive operating leverage" as a strategic pillar for 2025 growth.
  • Revenue Diversification: Business segments showed strength in areas like Markets (12% YoY revenue growth) and USPB (2% QoQ revenue increase) amid defensive performance in Wealth (24% YoY growth) and Services (3% YoY growth).

**Macroeconomic Trends Impacting 2025 Performance:**

  • U.S. Economic Resilience: CEO Fraser underscored the U.S.'s role as the "world's leading economy" and the dollar's purchasing power as long-term strategic advantages, despite near-term trade imbalances.
  • Global FX Volatility: Currency depreciation (e.g., Mexican peso) and higher revenue share impacted segments like Services and Markets, requiring hedging and portfolio repositioning.
  • Credit Conditions: Steeling for macroeconomic stress, Citi increased ACL builds in 1Q2025 ($210M net build) due to deteriorating macro outlook. Credit losses in card portfolios rose 6% YoY, though NPLs decreased 2% YoY via measured loan growth and diversification.
