Factors Driving NIM Changes

  • Consumer Loans: Rate-sensitive margins lifted by 2023 rate peaks (structurally sticky portfolio)
  • Commercial Books: Pessimistic macro outlook driving competitive pricing (cyclical compression)
  • Mortgages: 2022 borrowers repricing at lower rates (rate cycle headwind)
  • Wholesale Funding: BOrrowed money costs rose 12% QoQ (+$50M interest expense)

Aggregate NIM Performance

Total NIM (Q1'25): 2.71% — down from 2.85% in 4Q'24
Reason: Commercial margin compression (-14bps) + wholesale spreads (+9bps) offset by consumer strength (+27bps)
Funding Mix Impact: $4.2B shift to cheaper deposits vs 4Q
