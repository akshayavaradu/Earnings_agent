Qualitative Summary: Key Drivers of 1Q2025 Financial Performance

Net Interest Income: The 4% increase in net interest income (from \$4.6B to \$4.8B) was driven by strong contributions from USPB (6% growth due to higher deposit spreads and loan growth in Branded Cards) and Markets (7% growth from increased client activity and monetization). However, declines in Banking and All Other segments offset some of this growth. Notably, the overall rise in net interest income reflects Citi's focus on optimizing its business mix and leveraging macroeconomic conditions.

Non-Interest Income: The 1% increase in non-interest income (from \$17.4B to \$16.8B) was primarily due to gains in Markets (12% growth from equity derivatives and higher volatility) and Banking (12% from Investment Banking fees and prime services). However, declines in USPB and Services significantly impacted this segment. The reduction in non-interest income compared to prior-year performance highlights the challenges faced by certain businesses, including FX headwinds and episodic fee reductions.

Expenses: Operating expenses fell by 5% (from \$14.1B to \$13.4B) due to a smaller FDIC special assessment, absence of a restructuring charge, and productivity savings. The lower compensation expenses (including favorable FX impacts and cost reductions) were a key driver. However, expenses increased in technology and advertising as the bank invested in digital transformation and client engagement. When excluding these factors, expenses declined by 3%, underscoring cost discipline.

Key Context: Despite a 15% rise in cost of credit (\$2.7B in 1Q2025 vs. \$2.365B in 1Q2024), the combination of revenue growth (3%) and expense reduction (5%) led to a \$700M increase in net income (\$4.1B vs. \$3.4B). Strategic priorities like organizational simplification and focus on fee-based services drove this performance, even as macroeconomic risks (such as ACL build-ups and credit losses in legacy segments) persisted.
