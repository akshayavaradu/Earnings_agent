Key Observations

  • Markets: Largest contributor (5,986M), +12% YoY and +31% QoQ. Equity derivatives and prime services surged.
  • Investment Banking: Advisory fees spiked 84% as wallet share grew. ECM/DCM declined.
  • Declines:
    • Services non-interest revenue fell due to missing episodic fees in Securities Services.
    • USPB’s non-interest income dropped 168% (likely negative) due to partner payments.
  • Growth Drivers: Cross-border activity, client investment assets (+16%), equity volatility, and strong M&A volumes.

Qualitative Summary

Citigroup’s fee-based income showed a mixed performance in 1Q2025. Markets were the standout with double-digit growth, driven by volatile equity markets and increased client activity. Investment Banking fees rose significantly on strong advisory work, though ECM and DCM faced headwinds. Wealth Management fees grew steadily on investment fee gains, while Services and USPB faced headwinds from transactional revenue drops and partner payment costs. Markets and Investment Banking offset declines in other segments, reinforcing the need for多元化 in Citigroup’s business mix.
