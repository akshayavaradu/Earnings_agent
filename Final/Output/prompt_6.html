2. Qualitative Summary

Loan Portfolio Trends:

  • Branded Cards (USPB): Loan balances increased by 6% YoY, driven by higher card spend volume and interest-earning balances. The ACL release in 1Q2025 (-$171M) reduced delinquencies compared to a $246M build in 4Q2024, reflecting improved portfolio management and lower risk exposure.
  • Corporate Lending (Banking): Loans declined by 8% YoY, primarily due to lower loan balances and higher prior-year recoveries. The $185M ACL build in 1Q2025 indicates increased provisioning for macroeconomic risks, despite a 44% QoQ revenue growth from mark-to-market adjustments.
  • Services: Loan growth of 6% YoY was supported by Treasury and Trade Solutions (TTS) market share gains. A modest $18M ACL build suggests stable credit quality, though non-interest revenue fell 29% QoQ due to FX impacts and revenue-sharing adjustments.
  • Markets: Loans rose 7% YoY, driven by higher trading account assets and client activity. However, net credit losses increased 82% YoY, reflecting heightened risk in spread products, partially offset by a 52% decline in ACL builds compared to 1Q2024.
  • Wealth: Slight 1% YoY loan decline was offset by higher deposit spreads and investment fee revenues. A $60M ACL build in 1Q2025 highlights cautious provisioning amid macroeconomic uncertainty, despite a 24% YoY revenue increase.
Key Drivers: - Origination growth in Branded Cards and Markets was offset by declines in Banking. - Delinquencies improved in Corporate Lending and Services but worsened in Markets. - Macro factors (e.g., U.S. economic resilience, FX volatility) influenced ACL adjustments and credit losses across segments.
